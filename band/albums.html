My Favorite Albums

Bee Gees Greatest (1979)

The Bee Gees Greatest hit album became one of the most recognized albums of their career. It's one of those albums that make you want to get out of your seat and just dance and sing along with the trio.
It was also the forerunner to SATURDAY NIGHT FEVER. However, out of all the albums they've made together, this one is one of the most loved albums that the Bee Gees have made.

Saturday Night Fever (1977)

The Saturday Night Fever album is one of the best-selling albums in the international market. They sold on average about 40 million copies worldwide. This disco-pop album was full of dearly loved songs such as "Stayin Alive", "How deep is your love?", and "More than a Woman"
These few select songs from the Saturday Night Fever were a huge hit for the Bee Gees. The album itself was one of the best-selling soundtracks of all time and was even awarded a Grammy in the year of 1979.

Children of the World (1976)

The Children of the World album was a great album for its time. One of its song tracks "You should be dancing" was number one on this 10-track song album and even went on to become number one on the Billboard Hot 100 Chart. It was recorded by the same studio in Criteria Studios in Miami Florida, of which other Bee Gee albums were created. This album had an immense upbeat rhythm to its songs, which soon later resulted in it being praised as one of the most enjoyable light-hearted albums in Bee Gees career.
